Multivariate Statistics Project Nicholas Nason · Stonehill College · Spring 2026 BRFSS 2024 SAS Viya Directed Study n = 25,000 Project Goal About the Data Data Prep t-Test Factor Analysis Regression ANOVA Logistic Regression Conclusion Project Goal: Utilizing survey results from The Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) to examine whether behavioral, demographic, and social determinant factors are associated with poor physical and mental health outcomes among U.S. Adults, and to evaluate whether these factors can predict an individual's risk of chronic diseases. About the Data: The Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) is an annual telephone survey conducted by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) that collects data on health behaviors, chronic conditions, and preventive health practices from over 400,000 U.S. adults each year. This project uses a random sample of 25,000 BRFSS 2024 respondents to apply the multivariate statistical methods covered over the course of this directed study to a real-world public health dataset. The central research question is: what behavioral, demographic, and social determinant factors are associated with poor physical and mental health outcomes among U.S. adults, and can those factors be used to predict and classify chronic disease risk? This framing allows each statistical technique to contribute a distinct and meaningful angle to a single unified investigation rather than treating each method as an isolated exercise. Six analytical techniques are applied across six phases. The work begins with hypothesis testing to establish baseline relationships in the data, moves through factor analysis to uncover latent structure among social determinant variables, applies multiple linear regression and ANOVA/MANOVA to model and compare group differences in health outcomes, uses logistic regression to predict diabetes risk, and optionally applies discriminant analysis to classify general health status. Each analysis section follows a consistent write-up format: objective, variables, hypotheses, statistical procedure, results, interpretation, and diagnostic checks. The SAS code for all analyses is contained in the companion file BRFSS_Analysis_Code.sas and should be run in SAS Viya Workbench.
